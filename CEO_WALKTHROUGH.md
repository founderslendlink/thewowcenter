# WOW Center Miami — CEO Walkthrough Guide
## Website Review · May 2026

This document is a structured script and reference for walking WOW Center leadership through the new website. Use it before, during, or after any stakeholder demo session.

---

## How to Use This Document

1. **Before the demo** — Read "What to expect" and "Known gaps" so you can set the right framing.
2. **During the demo** — Follow the page-by-page walkthrough. The "Say this" lines are ready-to-use talking points.
3. **After the demo** — Use the "Items needing leadership input" checklist to collect decisions.

---

## What to Expect

This is a **design-complete, content-safe prototype**. Every page is live and navigable. The site is built on Next.js with full WCAG 2.2 AA accessibility infrastructure already in place.

**What is real:** Structure, navigation, design system, copy framework, links to external systems (Qgiv, Shopify, Asana forms).

**What is placeholder:** Photos (gradient backgrounds stand in for real photography), some content items flagged below, partner logos (awaiting confirmation), and campaign metrics (awaiting leadership sign-off).

**What is NOT on this site:** Payment processing, form submissions, stored data, or anything that touches a real server.

## Page-by-Page Walkthrough

### 1. Homepage — `/`

**Say this:**
> "The homepage opens with a strong mission statement and a clear call to action. Notice we have Apply for Admissions and Support Our Work — those are the two highest-priority actions for any visitor. Scroll down and you'll see three stats that confirm WOW's scale: 50+ years, 200+ members, 12 programs. Then we go right into the Programs section, the origin story, a donate prompt, and the admissions call to action. Nothing invented, nothing speculative."

**Key decisions needed:**
- [ ] Hero photo: Provide 1–2 photos of members in programs (art, culinary, outdoor). Min 1920×1080px.
- [ ] Do the three stats (50+ years, 200+ members, 12 programs) accurately reflect WOW Center today?

---

### 2. Programs — `/programs`

**Say this:**
> "Each of WOW's 12 programs has a dedicated page with a description, who it's for, and expected outcomes. These are built from the information in the digital audit — but every word can be edited once Sanity CMS is wired up. The CEO or program directors can update them without touching code."

**Key decisions needed:**
- [ ] Review each program page description for accuracy. Flag any that need revision.
- [ ] Are all 12 program names, descriptions, and outcomes accurate?

---

### 3. Donate — `/donate`

**Say this:**
> "The donation page is a persuasion page — it explains what gifts support, shows four giving levels with concrete impact, and then sends donors to WOW Center's existing Qgiv/Bloomerang platform via a 'Donate Securely' button. No payment information is collected on this site. We removed the form embed placeholder so this page looks clean and professional for the CEO walkthrough."

**Key decisions needed:**
- [ ] Confirm the Qgiv/Bloomerang donation page URL to wire up the "Donate Securely" button.
- [ ] Confirm the four giving levels ($25/$50/$100/$250) and their impact descriptions are accurate.

---

### 4. Building on a Dream — `/donate/building-on-a-dream`

**Say this:**
> "The capital campaign page exists and is fully designed. We intentionally left the progress bar as a placeholder — the $1.25M raised / $5M goal figures were not confirmed, so we replaced them with a note directing visitors to contact giving@wowcentermiami.org. The named giving opportunities ($5K–$500K) are also placeholder tiers that need your sign-off."

**Key decisions needed:**
- [ ] Campaign goal amount: $___M
- [ ] Amount raised to date: $___M (or leave as "contact us" if not public)
- [ ] Named giving tier amounts and labels — confirm or revise
- [ ] Major gift contact name and email

---

### 5. Admissions — `/admissions`

**Say this:**
> "The admissions hub describes the process, links to WOW's existing Asana inquiry form, and offers a phone number for direct contact. There's also a tour page and an FAQ page with 8 common questions. No family data is collected through this website — everything goes to the existing admissions form."

**Key decisions needed:**
- [ ] Confirm the Asana admissions form URL.
- [ ] Review the 8 FAQ questions and answers for accuracy.
- [ ] Is the admissions phone number correct: (305) 726-1766?

---

### 6. About — `/about`

**Say this:**
> "The About section has four pages: Our Story, Leadership, Board, and Impact. The story page covers WOW's founding in 1972 and major milestones — those are based on publicly available history and should be reviewed for accuracy. The leadership and board pages use placeholder bios and titles — no real names yet. The impact page shows four confirmed metrics and describes programs in general terms, with no invented statistics."

**Key decisions needed:**
- [ ] Leadership team: Provide names, titles, headshots, and short bios.
- [ ] Board of Directors: Provide names and titles (bios and headshots optional).
- [ ] Historical timeline on Our Story page: Confirm the key dates are accurate.
- [ ] Are the four impact stats (200+ members, 12 programs, 50+ years, 1:4 ratio) accurate?

---

### 7. The WOW Experience (Annual Gala) — `/events/wow-experience`

**Say this:**
> "The gala page is designed and ready. We removed the November 8 date and JW Marriott venue since those were not confirmed. It currently says 'Date & time to be announced' and 'Venue to be announced.' Sponsorship tiers are placeholder amounts. The ticket CTA links to the general contact form."

**Key decisions needed:**
- [ ] 2026 gala date, time, and venue
- [ ] Sponsorship package names, amounts, and perks
- [ ] Ticket pricing and purchase mechanism (external ticketing platform URL)

---

### 8. Shop — `/shop`

**Say this:**
> "The shop page links directly to WOW Center's existing Shopify store. No products are hosted here — it's a bridge page. Clean, simple, CEO-safe."

**Key decisions needed:**
- [ ] Confirm the Shopify store URL.

---

### 9. Partners / Volunteer — `/partners`, `/partners/volunteer`

**Say this:**
> "Partner and volunteer pages link to WOW Center's existing Asana form. The partner logos section on the homepage is currently a placeholder — we need WOW Center to confirm which partner and employer names/logos are approved to display publicly."

**Key decisions needed:**
- [ ] Approved list of partner and employer logos to display on the homepage.
- [ ] Confirm the volunteer/partner interest form URL (Asana).

---

### 10. Accessibility — `/accessibility`

**Say this:**
> "WOW Center's website is built with WCAG 2.2 AA as its accessibility foundation — not as an afterthought. The accessibility statement page lists every measure taken. This is meaningful for WOW's brand: a nonprofit serving adults with disabilities that has an inaccessible website is a credibility problem. We've eliminated that risk."

**No decisions needed** — this page is complete.

## Design System Reference

The site uses WOW Center's existing brand colors:

| Token | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Moss | `#2C5F2E` | Primary brand green, buttons, headings |
| Moss Deep | `#1B3A1D` | Dark sections, hero background |
| Amber | `#C86B1E` | Accent, CTAs, eyebrow labels |
| Cream | `#F5EFE3` | Page backgrounds, cards |
| Ink | `#1A1A1A` | Primary text |
| Ink-70 | `#4D4D4D` | Body copy, secondary text |

Typography: **Source Serif 4** (headlines) + **Manrope** (UI, navigation, labels)
